Learn how to use KwaCart

A step-by-step guide to everything the platform can do — from creating your account to downloading and sharing your results.

Chapter 1 🔐

Register — create your free account

Creating an account takes less than a minute. All you need is an email address and a password you choose yourself. No credit card. No waiting list. Just register and start.

  1. 1
    Go to the KwaCart home page

    Open your browser and go to the KwaCart website. You will see the home page with a big "Register now" button.

  2. 2
    Click "Register now"

    This takes you to the registration form. It is a short form — just two fields.

  3. 3
    Enter your email address

    Type the email address you want to use for your account. This is how you will log in every time.

  4. 4
    Choose a password

    Create a password you will remember. Type it once, then type it again in the second box to confirm it.

  5. 5
    Click "Register" — you are in

    Your account is created immediately. You will be taken to the log-in page. Enter your email and password, and you can start using KwaCart straight away.

💡
Already have an account?

Click "Log in" at the top of any page. Enter your email and password. You can update your email address or change your password at any time from your Profile page (the "Profile" link in the top navigation when you are logged in).

Chapter 2 🗺️

Blaze your own trail — find your pathway

KwaCart has 23 Liberating Structures tools. Not sure where to start? The Pathway Finder asks you three quick questions and matches you to the right tools for your situation — your time, your group, and your goals.

  1. 1
    Go to "Find a Pathway"

    Click "Find a Pathway" in the top navigation. You can also reach it from the tool catalog.

  2. 2
    How much time do you have?

    Choose how long your session will be — for example, 15 minutes, 30 minutes, or an hour. The Pathway Finder will only suggest tools that fit your time.

  3. 3
    How big is your group?

    Tell the tool how many people will be taking part. Some tools are designed for pairs; others work best with larger groups.

  4. 4
    What are your goals?

    Choose what you want to achieve — for example, understanding a problem, making a decision, sharing knowledge, or planning actions.

  5. 5
    See your matched tools

    KwaCart shows you the tools that best match your answers. Each result includes a short description, the time needed, and a button to launch it straight away.

🎯
Why use the Pathway Finder?

It takes the guesswork out of choosing. Rather than reading through all 23 tools, you answer three questions and get a personalised shortlist. It is especially helpful if you are new to Liberating Structures or if you are preparing a session at short notice.

Chapter 3 ✍️

Work Solo — use tools at your own pace

Every tool in KwaCart can be used completely on your own. Solo mode lets you think through a structured exercise in your own time, without a group or a timer pressing you. Your draft is saved automatically as you type, so you can come back to it whenever you are ready.

  1. 1
    Open the Tool Catalog

    Click "Work Solo" in the top navigation, or go to All Tools and filter by "Solo". You will see all the tools available to you.

  2. 2
    Choose your tool

    Each tool card shows a short tagline. Click on a tool to read a full description, then click "Work Solo" to open the drafting editor.

  3. 3
    Fill in your responses

    The tool guides you through each question or prompt. Type your answers in the text boxes provided. Your work is saved automatically — you will not lose anything if you close the tab.

  4. 4
    Submit your work

    When you are finished, click "Submit". KwaCart generates a formatted summary of your responses and saves it to your Knowledge Bank.

  5. 5
    Download or continue later

    Your submission appears in your Knowledge Bank. You can download it as a Markdown or RTF file at any time.

🧠
Why working solo matters

Solo mode is not just a fallback for when you have no group. It is a powerful practice in its own right. Working through a structured tool by yourself builds discipline — the habit of thinking carefully before speaking. It builds self-awareness — you discover what you actually think, not what you say in the moment under social pressure. It builds control — you develop the skill of structuring your ideas clearly, which makes you more effective when you do work with others. Many experienced facilitators complete tools solo before running them with a group, so they fully understand what they are asking others to do.

🏋️

Build a thinking habit

Use a tool once a week to reflect on your work and strengthen your thinking skills over time.

📝

Prepare for a meeting

Work through a tool before a group session so your contributions are clear, concrete, and ready to share.

🔍

Explore a problem

Use a structured prompt to think around a challenge from every angle — by yourself, without distraction.

🗂️

Build an archive

Each solo submission is saved automatically. Over time your Knowledge Bank becomes a record of your thinking and growth.

♿
Using AAC equipment in Solo mode

All text boxes in solo tools are standard browser inputs, which means they work with any AAC software that produces keyboard output — including Grid 3, Snap Core First, Proloquo2Go, and Clicker 8. There is no time pressure in solo mode, so you can compose at whatever pace suits you. See Chapter 5 for the full guide to AAC features.

Chapter 4 👥

Facilitate a group — live sessions with your team

Live sessions are where KwaCart really shines. You start a session as the host, share a code with your participants, and everyone works through the tool together in their browser — no app to download, no account needed for participants.

  1. 1
    Choose a tool and click "Facilitate"

    From the Tool Catalog, find the tool you want to use and click the "Facilitate" button. You will be taken to the session set-up page.

  2. 2
    Share the pairing code with your participants

    A short code appears on your screen. Tell participants to go to kwacart.com/join and type the code, or share the direct link shown on the session page. Participants can join from any device with a browser — no sign-up needed.

  3. 3
    Watch participants join

    As each person joins, you can see them appear on your host screen. Wait until everyone is in before starting the timer.

  4. 4
    Start the timer and let people work

    Click "Start timer" to begin the session. A built-in timer counts down for each phase of the tool. Participants type their responses in their own browser. Their work is saved automatically as they type.

  5. 5
    Close the session when everyone is done

    When the time is up (or when you are ready), click "Close session". This locks all contributions and generates the combined results. Participants can no longer add or change their responses after this point.

  6. 6
    Review the combined results

    You are taken to the session summary page where all contributions are shown together. From here you can preview the formatted output and download the combined summary file.

⏱️
Timer and pacing controls

As the host, you control the timer. You can start it, reset it, and adjust the pace. KwaCart also supports Inclusive Pacing — a personal time multiplier for participants who use AAC devices or need extra time to compose their responses. A Verbal Breakout signal lets you tell the room to move to spoken discussion while AAC users continue typing digitally. See Chapter 5 for a full guide to all AAC features available during live sessions.

Session tools available to hosts

🔢

Pairing code

A short code so guests can join at kwacart.com/join — no account needed.

⏳

Built-in timer

A calm countdown timer with colour phases — green, amber, red — so everyone knows how much time is left.

✏️

Live composition flag

A subtle indicator appears next to a participant when they are actively typing, even before they save — so you know not to close the session too early.

🖼️

Multimedia input

Participants who use symbol boards or AAC apps can upload an image or audio clip as their response alongside written text.

📥

Instant download

After closing, download the combined session output as a Markdown or RTF file immediately.

🗄️

Session archive

Every closed session is saved to your Knowledge Bank. You can reopen and download at any time.

🛑
Ending vs deleting a session

Closing a session locks contributions and generates the summary — participants cannot change their responses after this. The session stays in your Knowledge Bank permanently unless you choose to delete it. Deleting a session removes it and all its data permanently. This cannot be undone. Only the host can close or delete a session.

Chapter 5 ♿

AAC features — built for every voice

KwaCart is designed to work with Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) devices and software. Whether you communicate using eye gaze, switch scanning, symbol boards, or a voice-output app, the platform includes dedicated features to make participation equal, comfortable, and pressure-free.

{# ── Input methods ── #}

Ways to add your response

⌨️

Typed text — via any keyboard or AAC software

Every response field in KwaCart is a standard browser text area. Any AAC software that generates keyboard output will work with it automatically — including Grid 3, Snap Core First, Proloquo2Go, TouchChat, Communicator 5, and Clicker 8. You do not need to set anything up. Open the session page, focus the text box, and your AAC software will type into it as normal. On-screen keyboards (including the built-in Windows and iOS keyboards) also work without any configuration.

🎙️

Audio clip — record or upload a voice message

During a live group session, participants can attach an audio clip alongside their written text. This is useful when a person uses voice output from their AAC device and wants to capture the spoken message as part of their contribution. To attach audio: find the attachment section on your participant page, click "Upload audio", and select an audio file from your device (MP3, WAV, M4A, and OGG are supported). The audio is stored with your response and included in the session archive for the facilitator to review in the Synthesis Workspace.

🖼️

Image — upload a symbol board or photograph

Participants who communicate using symbol boards, Picture Exchange Communication Systems (PECS), or drawn images can upload a photo or screenshot of their board as part of their session response. To attach an image: find the attachment section on your participant page, click "Upload image", and select a JPG, PNG, or WebP file. The image is saved with your response and shown to the facilitator in the Synthesis Workspace, where they can add a text transcription before generating the final combined summary.

{# ── Timer features ── #}

Timer features designed for AAC users

🟢

Calm Timer

Instead of flashing digits counting down, the Calm Timer shows a slow, gentle colour drift — green means plenty of time, amber means it is getting shorter, red means nearly done. No sudden changes. No alarming ticking. Just a quiet, readable signal that works for people who find rapidly changing numbers distracting or anxiety-inducing.

✏️

Composition Presence Flag

When you are typing — even if your text box is currently empty — a small "I'm composing…" signal appears next to your name on the host's screen. This tells the facilitator that you are still working, so they will not close the session before you have finished. AAC users often take longer to compose a message, and this flag protects your time without you having to ask.

⏳

Inclusive Pacing

The host can switch on Inclusive Pacing for any participant. This gives that person a personal time multiplier — for example, double time — so the session timer does not feel like a barrier. The rest of the group sees the normal timer; only the paced participant sees the adjusted countdown.

🗣️

Verbal Breakout

When the host signals a Verbal Breakout, the room moves to spoken discussion — but AAC participants see a clear on-screen message telling them they can continue composing digitally for as long as they need. Their written contributions will be included in the final summary alongside the spoken discussion notes.

{# ── Accessibility Mode ── #}

Accessibility Mode

🔆
How to turn it on

Click the "Accessibility Mode" button in the top navigation bar (visible on every page, including the session page). When it is on, KwaCart switches to a layout optimised for switch scanning, eye gaze, and AAC users:

🎯

Large targets

All buttons and interactive elements become larger and further apart — easier to select with eye gaze, a head pointer, or a switch.

🧊

Frozen layout

Nothing moves or shifts unexpectedly. The page layout is locked so that scanning grids stay in the positions your device expects.

⏸️

No animations

All transitions, fades, and motion effects are disabled. The interface is completely still — better for people who find motion distracting or distressing.

💾

Remembered between visits

Your preference is saved in your browser. Accessibility Mode stays on until you turn it off — you do not have to set it again each visit.

{# ── Connecting AAC equipment ── #}

Connecting your AAC equipment

KwaCart runs entirely in a web browser, so it works with any AAC hardware or software that can control a browser on a computer, tablet, or phone. You do not need to install a special app or configure KwaCart — just set up your equipment to control the browser as you normally would, open KwaCart, and it works.

  1. 1
    Set up your AAC device or software as usual

    Configure your eye gaze system, switch scanner, or AAC app on your device exactly as you would for any other browser-based activity. No special settings for KwaCart are needed.

  2. 2
    Open a browser and go to KwaCart

    Use whatever input method you use to control your browser — eye gaze cursor, switch scanning to the address bar, or your AAC app's browser control feature.

  3. 3
    Turn on Accessibility Mode

    Press the "Accessibility Mode" button in the navigation bar. This makes all targets larger and removes all animations, which makes switch scanning and eye gaze selection much easier.

  4. 4
    Focus a text box and start composing

    Select a response field with your input method. Your AAC software will type into it exactly as it would in any other text box. Your work is saved automatically as you go.

  5. 5
    Tell your facilitator if you need more time

    Ask your facilitator to switch on Inclusive Pacing for you at the start of the session. This gives you a personal time extension so the session timer does not cut you off before you are done.

Chapter 6 🏦

The Knowledge Bank — your archive of everything

Every solo submission and every closed group session is saved automatically to your Knowledge Bank. Think of it as your personal library of structured thinking — organised by tool, searchable, and always available to download.

  1. 1
    Click "Knowledge Bank" in the top navigation

    This takes you to your personal archive. You will see a list of all the tools you have used, with a count of how many submissions or sessions you have saved for each one.

  2. 2
    Click on a tool to see your activity

    You will see two tables — one for solo submissions and one for group sessions. Each row shows the date, the status, and a set of action buttons.

  3. 3
    Preview a summary in the browser

    Click "Preview" next to any submission or session. A formatted preview opens in a panel on the same page — you can read through the full output before downloading.

  4. 4
    Download your file

    From the preview panel, click "Download" to save the file to your device. You can choose Markdown (.md) or RTF format depending on what you want to do with it next.

Two download formats — what is the difference?

.md

Markdown

Plain text with simple formatting marks (# for headings, ** for bold). Readable in any text editor. Best for uploading to AI tools, version control systems, or static site generators.

.rtf

Rich Text Format

A formatted document with headings, bold, and layout already applied. Opens directly in Microsoft Word, Apple Pages, LibreOffice, and most desktop publishing tools — ready to edit immediately.

What can you do with your files once you have downloaded them?

🤖

Upload to an AI tool for knowledge development

Paste your Markdown file into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any other AI assistant. Ask it to summarise your session, identify themes across multiple submissions, suggest follow-up actions, draft a report from the raw data, or turn the group's ideas into a formatted action plan. The structured output from KwaCart is ideal for AI input — it is already organised and labelled, so the AI can understand it quickly.

🖥️

Use with desktop publishing software

Import your RTF file into Microsoft Publisher, Adobe InDesign, Affinity Publisher, or Canva. Use the content to build newsletters, reports, handouts, training materials, or grant applications — with professional layout and design. The structured headings and bullet points from KwaCart map cleanly to most publishing templates.

📄

Further develop with word processing software

Open your RTF file in Microsoft Word, Apple Pages, Google Docs, or LibreOffice Writer. Edit the text, add context, combine multiple session summaries into a longer document, or use it as the basis of a report or proposal. Everything you need to add narrative, commentary, and recommendations around your group's structured output.

📊

Build a learning or development portfolio

Save and organise your Knowledge Bank downloads in a folder on your device. Over time they become a portfolio of evidence — showing how your thinking, your team's practice, or your organisation's knowledge has grown. Useful for CPD records, supervision reports, grant applications, and staff appraisals.

🤝

Share with your team or stakeholders

Send the RTF or Markdown file by email, upload it to a shared drive (Google Drive, SharePoint, Dropbox), or paste the content into a team workspace (Notion, Confluence, Slack). Your group's structured output is immediately readable and useful to anyone who receives it — no KwaCart account required.

🔒
Your data is private to you

Only you can see your solo submissions and the sessions you hosted. Nobody else's data appears in your Knowledge Bank. Staff members can see account-level information for support purposes, but your session content is visible only to the people in your session.
