Yes, Cloudinary can support .md and .rtf files, as long as they’re not on the blocked list. They’ll be uploaded as raw assets (stored as-is), so you can deliver them via a URL, but you won’t be able to transform them. (https://cloudinary.com/documentation/upload_parameters) (https://cloudinary.com/documentation/cloudinary_glossary) 
 The only formats that are rejected by default are certain executable and potentially harmful file types (like .exe, .apk, .bat, .sh, etc.). (https://cloudinary.com/documentation/ts_why_does_cloudinary_reject_the_file_format_of_some_files_im_uploading) 
 So .md and .rtf should be fine to upload and deliver as raw files.
